Why Donate?

With your help, Pakistani local communities and refugees can get immediate assistance including shelter, basic goods, and blankets.

Donation Links

Islamic Relief

Since 1992, Islamic Relief has been active in Pakistan. The organization's primary goals are to provide access to clean water, food assistance, and other humanitarian items.

UNICEF

The United Nations Children's Fund is striving to offer impacted families hygiene kits, water, and health services. The organization is also establishing transitory educational facilities.

International Medical Corps

Since 1985, the International Medical Corps has been conducting operations there. The organization's main goals are to provide services for water, sanitation, and hygiene as well as medical treatment, supplies, and assistance for mental health.

Alkhidmat Foundation Pakistan

A fundraising drive for an emergency flood was started by this nonprofit. Since July, the group has been giving individuals in need food and shelter.

International Rescue Committee

Since 1980, the IRC has been active in Pakistan. Nearly 20,000 people have received life-saving food, supplies, and medical assistance from the group.

Mercy Corps

Baluchistan is a hard-hit area, and Mercy Corps is working there to help those in need by giving them food, water, and money.

Save the Children

Since 1979, Save the Children has operated in Pakistan. The group is supplying afflicted regions with shelter, education, food, and kitchenware.

Humanity & Inclusion

With an emphasis on the elderly and those with disabilities, they are distributing food and other essentials throughout Pakistan.

Muslim Aid Pakistan

With assistance from Muslim Aid USA, UK, and the Start Network, they have set up their own flood relief fund. According to the non-governmental group, 29,325 Pakistanis have received assistance to date.
